Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon, Iceland

Why should I travel here?

Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon in Iceland is unique in many ways. First, it is located next to Europe's largest glacier, Vatnajokull. Next, within Iceland, there is only 3 national parks, and Jokulsarlon is part of one, characterized by its ice, highlands, lava landscape, and glaciers. Next, this lagoon is a prime demonstration of global warming, as every year the ice melts, making the lagoon bigger and bigger. This makes each visit different and special. Finally,Jokulsarlon offers something most beaches cannot: black sand. Although the sand is covered by translucent ice, the beach still has gleaming black sand underneath the pure ice.

Additional Information about the Lagoon

Besides the geographical context of the Lagoon, Jokulsarlon also features a lot of wildlife, including seals, birds, and sometimes puffins! In order to see this amazing Iceland resource, one must drive from the closest city of Reykjavik or go on a tour to the lagoon. From Reykjavik, it is a scenic 6 hour drive, featuring waterfalls, a black beach, and the Eyjafjallajokull volcano. Tours include going on an amphibian boat around the lake(takes a few hours), participating in a glacier hike, or going ice caving from the natural blue cave within the Vatnajokull glacier.The Lagoon offers a variety of activities for every age, so plan on visiting for more than one day to get the full experience of the wonders of Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon!

Other Activities to do Around the Lagoon

Besides the lagoon, Iceland has many other activities for all ages!

    Some of these activities include:
  • The Volcano Hekla, one of the most active volcanoes on the planet. This volcano has served inspiration for artists and film makers, making it a spectacular spot to visit in Iceland!
  • Seljavallalaug Pool, one of the oldest outdoor swimming pools in Iceland, is built on a wet hillside with naturally warm water that gushes from the rock face of a mountain. Plus, it is only a two hour drive from Reykjavik!
  • The Northern Lights can be seen from Reykjavik during the months of September to April, making for a picture-perfect activity for the whole family!
